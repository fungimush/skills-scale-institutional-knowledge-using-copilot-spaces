## Communication Cadence Calendar

### Daily
- Team standup (internal team)

### Weekly
- Status update email to stakeholders
- Cross-team coordination sync (if dependencies exist)
- Risk register review

### Bi-Weekly
- Sprint review/demo
- Leadership briefing

### Monthly
- Stakeholder town hall or Q&A
- Metrics review and trend analysis

### Milestone-Based
- Milestone achievement announcement
- Phase transition meeting
- Release announcement

### As-Needed
- Risk escalation
- Breaking change notice (60+ days before change)
- Incident communication
- Decision request

## Escalation Matrix

### Level 1: Team-Level
- **Trigger**: Day-to-day questions, minor blockers
- **Response Time**: Within 1 business day
- **Contact**: Project Manager or Communications Lead

### Level 2: Management-Level
- **Trigger**: Timeline risks, resource constraints, cross-team conflicts
- **Response Time**: Within 4 hours
- **Contact**: Product Lead, Engineering Manager

### Level 3: Executive-Level
- **Trigger**: Major scope changes, budget overruns, business-impacting issues
- **Response Time**: Immediate (within 1 hour)
- **Contact**: VP/Director, Sponsor

## Communication Effectiveness Metrics

Track these metrics to improve stakeholder communication:
- **Response Time**: Average time to respond to stakeholder questions
- **Meeting Attendance**: Stakeholder participation in key meetings
- **Feedback Collection**: Regular stakeholder satisfaction surveys
- **Communication Reach**: Percentage of stakeholders receiving communications
- **Clarity Score**: Feedback on communication clarity and usefulness

## Tips for Effective Stakeholder Communication

### Do's
- Keep messages concise and action-oriented
- Use consistent formatting and subject lines
- Highlight decisions needed and deadlines clearly
- Provide context but focus on what matters to each audience
- Follow up on commitments made in communications
- Use visuals (dashboards, charts) to convey complex information

### Don'ts
- Don't surprise stakeholders with bad news—communicate risks early
- Don't use jargon with non-technical audiences
- Don't over-communicate—respect stakeholder time
- Don't forget to close the loop on resolved issues
- Don't assume everyone reads every message—use multiple channels for critical info
